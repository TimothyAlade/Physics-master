Chapter 4: Motion

📘 SS1 First Term ⏱️ Reading: 35 mins

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Define distance, displacement, speed, velocity, and acceleration
  • Distinguish between distance and displacement
  • Distinguish between speed and velocity
  • Apply the equations of uniformly accelerated motion
  • Interpret motion graphs (distance-time, velocity-time)
  • Solve problems involving motion

4.1 Distance and Displacement

📌 Distance: The total length of the path traveled by an object. It is a scalar quantity.
📌 Displacement: The change in position of an object in a specific direction. It is a vector quantity.
📐 Example: A person walks 4 km East, then 3 km North.
Distance = 4 km + 3 km = 7 km
Displacement = √(4² + 3²) = √25 = 5 km in the Northeast direction
DistanceDisplacement
Scalar quantityVector quantity
Always positiveCan be positive, negative, or zero
Depends on the pathDepends only on start and end points
Distance ≥ DisplacementDisplacement ≤ Distance

4.2 Speed and Velocity

📌 Speed: The rate of change of distance. Scalar quantity.
Speed = Distance ÷ Time
📌 Velocity: The rate of change of displacement. Vector quantity.
Velocity = Displacement ÷ Time
📐 Example: A car travels 100 km North in 2 hours.
Speed = 100 km ÷ 2 h = 50 km/h
Velocity = 100 km North ÷ 2 h = 50 km/h North
SpeedVelocity
Scalar (magnitude only)Vector (magnitude + direction)
Always positiveCan be positive or negative
Speed = distance/timeVelocity = displacement/time

4.3 Acceleration

📌 Acceleration: The rate of change of velocity. It is a vector quantity.
a = (v - u) ÷ t
Where: u = initial velocity, v = final velocity, t = time
Unit: m/s² (metres per second squared)
📐 Example: A car accelerates from 10 m/s to 30 m/s in 5 seconds.
a = (30 - 10) ÷ 5 = 20 ÷ 5 = 4 m/s²

Types of Acceleration:

  • Positive acceleration: Velocity increases (speeding up)
  • Negative acceleration (deceleration): Velocity decreases (slowing down)
  • Uniform acceleration: Constant rate of change of velocity
  • Acceleration due to gravity (g): 9.8 m/s² downward

4.4 Equations of Uniformly Accelerated Motion

These equations apply when acceleration is constant:

(1) v = u + at
(2) s = ut + ½at²
(3) v² = u² + 2as
(4) s = (u + v)t ÷ 2

Where:

  • u = initial velocity (m/s)
  • v = final velocity (m/s)
  • a = acceleration (m/s²)
  • t = time (s)
  • s = displacement/distance (m)
📐 Example 1: A car starts from rest and accelerates at 2 m/s² for 10 seconds. Find its final velocity and distance covered.

u = 0, a = 2 m/s², t = 10 s
v = u + at = 0 + (2 × 10) = 20 m/s
s = ut + ½at² = 0 + 0.5 × 2 × 100 = 100 m
📐 Example 2: A car traveling at 20 m/s brakes and comes to rest in 5 seconds. Find the deceleration and distance covered.

u = 20 m/s, v = 0, t = 5 s
a = (v - u)/t = (0 - 20)/5 = -4 m/s² (deceleration)
s = (u + v)t/2 = (20 + 0) × 5 / 2 = 50 m

Advanced Motion Problems (Premium)

Free Fall (Motion under Gravity)

For objects falling under gravity, a = g = 9.8 m/s² (downward).

v = u + gt
h = ut + ½gt²
v² = u² + 2gh

Example: A ball is dropped from a height of 20 m. Find the time to hit the ground and the impact velocity.

Solution:
u = 0, h = 20 m, g = 9.8 m/s²
h = ½gt² → 20 = 0.5 × 9.8 × t² → t² = 4.08 → t = 2.02 s
v = gt = 9.8 × 2.02 = 19.8 m/s

Projectile Motion Basics

A projectile has both horizontal and vertical components of motion.

Horizontal: constant velocity (ax = 0)
Vertical: accelerated motion (ay = g = 9.8 m/s²)

4.5 Graphs of Motion

Distance-Time Graphs

  • Straight line (sloped): Uniform speed
  • Horizontal line: Object at rest
  • Curved line: Acceleration or deceleration
  • Slope = Speed

Velocity-Time Graphs

  • Straight line (sloped): Uniform acceleration
  • Horizontal line: Constant velocity (zero acceleration)
  • Slope = Acceleration
  • Area under graph = Distance traveled
📊 Graph Summary:
• Distance-Time graph: Slope = Speed
• Velocity-Time graph: Slope = Acceleration
• Velocity-Time graph: Area = Distance

Graph Analysis Practice (Premium)

Q1: A velocity-time graph shows a straight line from (0,0) to (10,20). Find the acceleration and distance.

Solution:
Acceleration = slope = (20-0)/(10-0) = 2 m/s²
Distance = area of triangle = ½ × base × height = 0.5 × 10 × 20 = 100 m

Q2: A car travels at constant speed for 5s, then accelerates uniformly for 5s. Interpret the motion.

Solution: The velocity-time graph shows a horizontal line (constant velocity) followed by a sloped line (acceleration).

Chapter 5: Forces

📘 SS1 First Term ⏱️ Reading: 35 mins

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Define force and state its SI unit
  • Distinguish between contact and non-contact forces
  • Identify different types of forces
  • Calculate resultant force
  • Explain equilibrium of forces
  • Apply force concepts to solve problems

5.1 What is Force?

📌 Force: A push or pull that can change the state of motion, shape, or direction of an object.
SI Unit: Newton (N)
Formula: F = m × a

Force is a vector quantity—it has both magnitude and direction.

💡 1 Newton is the force required to accelerate a 1 kg mass at 1 m/s².

5.2 Types of Forces

📌 Contact Forces

Forces that require physical contact between objects:

Forces that act at a distance without physical contact:

5.3 Resultant Force

📌 Resultant Force: The single force that has the same effect as all the individual forces acting on an object combined.

Forces in the Same Direction

Fresultant = F₁ + F₂
📐 Example: Two people push a car with forces of 300 N and 200 N in the same direction.
Resultant = 300 N + 200 N = 500 N

Forces in Opposite Directions

Fresultant = F₁ - F₂ (in direction of larger force)
📐 Example: A tug-of-war: Team A pulls 500 N East, Team B pulls 350 N West.
Resultant = 500 N - 350 N = 150 N East

5.4 Equilibrium of Forces

📌 Equilibrium: A state where the resultant force on an object is zero.
• Static Equilibrium: Object at rest
• Dynamic Equilibrium: Object moving at constant velocity
⚖️ Condition for Equilibrium: ΣF = 0 (sum of all forces = zero)

5.5 Newton's Laws of Motion

Newton's First Law (Law of Inertia)

An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion with the same speed and direction, unless acted upon by an unbalanced force.

Newton's Second Law

F = m × a

The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force and inversely proportional to its mass.

📐 Example: A 10 kg object experiences a net force of 50 N. Find acceleration.
a = F/m = 50/10 = 5 m/s²

Newton's Third Law

For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.
📐 Example: Rocket propulsion—exhaust gases push downward, rocket moves upward.

5.7 Friction

📌 Friction: A force that opposes motion between two surfaces in contact.

Types of Friction

  • Static Friction: Prevents object from starting to move
  • Kinetic (Sliding) Friction: Opposes motion of moving object
  • Rolling Friction: Opposes rolling motion (less than sliding)
  • Fluid Friction: Resistance in liquids/gases
✅ Advantages of Friction: Walking, writing, braking, tires gripping road
❌ Disadvantages of Friction: Wear and tear, energy loss as heat

📝 Chapter 5 Summary

  • Force is a push or pull; SI unit: Newton (N)
  • Contact forces: Friction, Tension, Normal, Applied, Air resistance
  • Non-contact forces: Gravitational, Magnetic, Electrostatic, Nuclear
  • Resultant force: Add forces in same direction, subtract opposite
  • Newton's Laws: 1st (Inertia), 2nd (F=ma), 3rd (Action=Reaction)
  • Weight = mass × gravity (W = mg)

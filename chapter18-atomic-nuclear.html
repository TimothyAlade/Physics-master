18.1 Structure of the Atom

📌 Atom: The smallest unit of an element, consisting of a nucleus (protons + neutrons) surrounded by electrons.

Proton (p⁺)+1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C1.67 × 10⁻²⁷Nucleus

  • Atomic Number (Z): Number of protons
  • Mass Number (A): Number of protons + neutrons
  • Isotopes: Same Z, different A (same element, different neutrons)

18.2 Radioactivity

📌 Radioactivity: The spontaneous emission of radiation from unstable atomic nuclei.

18.3 Radioactive Decay

N = N₀ e^(−λt)

Where: N = number of nuclei remaining, N₀ = initial number, λ = decay constant, t = time

Half-Life (T½)

📌 Half-Life: The time taken for half the radioactive nuclei in a sample to decay.
T½ = ln(2) / λ = 0.693 / λ
📐 Example: A radioactive sample has half-life of 5 years. After 20 years, what fraction remains?
Number of half-lives = 20/5 = 4
Fraction remaining = (½)⁴ = 1/16

18.4 Nuclear Reactions

Nuclear Fission

📌 Nuclear Fission: Splitting of a heavy nucleus (e.g., Uranium-235) into two lighter nuclei, releasing large amounts of energy. Used in nuclear power plants and atomic bombs.

Nuclear Fusion

📌 Nuclear Fusion: Combining of two light nuclei (e.g., hydrogen isotopes) to form a heavier nucleus, releasing even more energy than fission. Occurs in the Sun and stars.
FissionFusion
Splits heavy nucleusCombines light nuclei
Produces radioactive wasteCleaner (less waste)
Used in nuclear reactorsStill experimental for power

18.5 Uses of Radioactivity

  • Medicine: Cancer treatment (radiotherapy), medical imaging (PET scans), sterilization of equipment
  • Industry: Thickness gauges, smoke detectors (Americium-241), detecting leaks in pipes
  • Agriculture: Food preservation (irradiation), pest control, studying plant growth
  • Archaeology: Carbon-14 dating to determine age of fossils and artifacts
  • Energy: Nuclear power plants generate electricity

18.6 Dangers of Radiation

  • Cell damage: Ionizing radiation damages DNA and cells
  • Radiation sickness: Nausea, vomiting, hair loss
  • Cancer: Long-term exposure increases cancer risk
  • Genetic mutations: Can affect future generations
  • Environmental contamination: Radioactive waste must be stored safely for thousands of years
☢️ Safety Precautions: Use shielding (lead for gamma), limit exposure time, maintain distance from source, use remote handling tools, wear protective clothing.

📝 Chapter 18 Summary

  • Atom: Protons(+), Neutrons(0), Electrons(−)
  • Radioactivity: Alpha(α), Beta(β), Gamma(γ) radiation
  • Half-Life: T½ = 0.693/λ
  • Fission: Splitting heavy nuclei (U-235)
  • Fusion: Combining light nuclei (H → He in stars)
  • Uses: Medicine, energy, archaeology, industry

20.1 Measurement Experiments

Reading Vernier Calipers

📌 Vernier Caliper: Accuracy = 0.01 cm or 0.1 mm
Reading = Main Scale + (Vernier Coincidence × 0.01 cm)
📐 Example: Main scale = 2.3 cm, Vernier coincidence = 4
Reading = 2.3 + (4 × 0.01) = 2.34 cm

Reading Micrometer Screw Gauge

📌 Micrometer: Accuracy = 0.01 mm or 0.001 cm
Reading = Main Scale + (Circular Scale × 0.01 mm)
📐 Example: Main scale = 5.5 mm, Circular scale = 28
Reading = 5.5 + (28 × 0.01) = 5.78 mm

20.2 Density Experiment

ρ = m / V

Apparatus: Beam balance, measuring cylinder, water, irregular solid (stone)

Procedure:

  1. Measure mass of stone using beam balance (m)
  2. Pour water into measuring cylinder, record initial volume (V₁)
  3. Gently lower stone into water, record final volume (V₂)
  4. Volume of stone = V₂ − V₁
  5. Calculate density: ρ = m / (V₂ − V₁)

20.3 Verification of Ohm's Law

V = IR

Apparatus: Battery, rheostat, ammeter, voltmeter, resistor, connecting wires

Procedure:

  1. Connect circuit with ammeter in series, voltmeter in parallel
  2. Adjust rheostat to vary current
  3. Record pairs of V and I readings
  4. Plot graph of V (y-axis) against I (x-axis)
  5. Straight line through origin confirms Ohm's Law
  6. Slope = Resistance (R)

20.4 Simple Pendulum Experiment

T = 2π√(L/g)  →  T² = (4π²/g)L

Apparatus: String, bob, clamp, stopwatch, metre rule

Procedure:

  1. Set pendulum length L (from pivot to center of bob)
  2. Displace bob slightly (< 10°) and release
  3. Time 20 complete oscillations → find period T
  4. Repeat for different lengths
  5. Plot graph of T² (y-axis) against L (x-axis)
  6. Slope = 4π²/g → calculate g

20.5 Focal Length of Convex Lens

1/f = 1/u + 1/v

Apparatus: Convex lens, screen, illuminated object, metre rule

Procedure:

  1. Place lens between object and screen
  2. Adjust until sharp image formed on screen
  3. Measure u (object to lens) and v (lens to screen)
  4. Repeat for different u values
  5. Calculate f using lens formula or plot 1/v vs 1/u

20.6 Graph Work and Data Analysis

📊 Graph Plotting Rules:
• Use appropriate scales (1 cm = 1, 2, 5, or 10 units)
• Label axes with quantity and unit
• Plot points with small crosses (×)
• Draw best-fit straight line or smooth curve
• Slope = Δy/Δx (use large triangle on line, NOT data points)
• Intercept = where line crosses axis (x=0 or y=0)

20.7 Common Practical Questions (WAEC/NECO)

Q1: In a pendulum experiment, the following data was obtained: L = 100 cm, time for 20 oscillations = 40 s. Calculate the period and use it to find g.
Answer: T = 40/20 = 2 s, T² = 4π²L/g → g = 4π²L/T² = 4×(3.14)²×1/4 = 9.86 m/s²
Q2: In an optics experiment, u = 30 cm and v = 60 cm. Calculate the focal length.
Answer: 1/f = 1/30 + 1/60 = 3/60 → f = 20 cm

20.8 Precautions in Experiments

  • Take multiple readings and calculate average
  • Avoid parallax error (eye directly above scale)
  • Check for zero error on instruments before use
  • Use rigid supports to prevent unnecessary movement
  • Ensure connections are tight in electrical experiments
  • Record readings to appropriate decimal places

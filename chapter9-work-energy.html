Chapter 9: Work, Energy and Power

📗 SS2 First Term ⏱️ Reading: 40 mins

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Define work and calculate work done
  • Define energy and identify different forms
  • State and apply the law of conservation of energy
  • Calculate kinetic and potential energy
  • Define power and calculate power output
  • Distinguish between work, energy, and power

9.1 Work

📌 Work: Work is done when a force moves an object in the direction of the force.
SI Unit: Joule (J)  |  1 J = 1 N × 1 m
W = F × d × cos θ

Where: W = work (J), F = force (N), d = distance (m), θ = angle between force and displacement

📐 Example 1: A force of 50 N pushes a box 10 m. Find work done.
W = 50 × 10 = 500 J (force parallel to displacement, cos 0° = 1)
📐 Example 2: A person lifts a 20 kg bag through a height of 2 m. Find work done against gravity.
F = mg = 20 × 9.8 = 196 N
W = 196 × 2 = 392 J
⚠️ When is NO work done?
• No displacement (pushing a wall that doesn't move)
• Force perpendicular to displacement (carrying a bag horizontally — gravity does no work)

9.2 Energy

📌 Energy: The capacity or ability to do work. SI Unit: Joule (J). Energy is a scalar quantity.

Forms of Energy

9.3 Kinetic Energy (KE)

📌 Kinetic Energy: Energy possessed by an object due to its motion.
KE = ½mv²

Where: m = mass (kg), v = velocity (m/s)

📐 Example: A car of mass 1000 kg moves at 20 m/s. Find its KE.
KE = 0.5 × 1000 × 20² = 0.5 × 1000 × 400 = 200,000 J (200 kJ)

9.4 Potential Energy (PE)

📌 Gravitational Potential Energy: Energy possessed by an object due to its position above the ground.
PE = mgh

Where: m = mass (kg), g = 9.8 m/s², h = height (m)

📐 Example: A 5 kg ball is held 10 m above ground. Find its PE.
PE = 5 × 9.8 × 10 = 490 J
🔄 PE to KE Conversion: As an object falls, PE decreases and KE increases. At any point: Total Energy = KE + PE (ignoring air resistance).

9.5 Conservation of Energy

📌 Law of Conservation of Energy: Energy cannot be created or destroyed; it can only be transformed from one form to another. The total energy in a closed system remains constant.
📐 Example (Pendulum): At highest point: Max PE, Zero KE. At lowest point: Zero PE, Max KE. Total energy remains constant.
📐 Example (Free Fall): A 2 kg ball dropped from 20 m. Find velocity just before hitting ground.
PE at top = KE at bottom
mgh = ½mv²
2 × 9.8 × 20 = 0.5 × 2 × v²
392 = v²
v = 19.8 m/s

9.6 Power

📌 Power: The rate of doing work or the rate of energy transfer. SI Unit: Watt (W). 1 W = 1 J/s.
P = W / t   or   P = E / t
P = F × v   (for constant velocity)
📐 Example 1: A machine does 5000 J of work in 10 seconds. Find power.
P = 5000 / 10 = 500 W
📐 Example 2: A car engine provides 2000 N force at 25 m/s. Find power.
P = F × v = 2000 × 25 = 50,000 W (50 kW)
QuantitySymbolSI UnitFormula
WorkWJoule (J)W = Fd
EnergyEJoule (J)KE = ½mv², PE = mgh
PowerPWatt (W)P = W/t = Fv

📝 Chapter 9 Summary

  • Work: W = F × d (Joules) — force × distance in direction of force
  • Kinetic Energy: KE = ½mv² — energy of motion
  • Potential Energy: PE = mgh — energy of position
  • Conservation of Energy: Energy transforms but total remains constant
  • Power: P = W/t (Watts) — rate of doing work
🎯 WAEC/NECO Tip: Work, energy, and power calculations appear in almost every exam! Master KE = ½mv², PE = mgh, and P = W/t.

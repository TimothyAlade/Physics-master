Chapter 2: Measurement

📘 SS1 First Term ⏱️ Reading: 30 mins

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Define measurement and its importance in physics
  • Distinguish between fundamental and derived quantities
  • State the 7 fundamental quantities and their SI units
  • Identify common measuring instruments
  • Explain measurement errors
  • Differentiate between accuracy and precision

2.1 What is Measurement?

📌 Measurement: The process of finding the size, amount, or quantity of something using numbers and standard units.

Without measurement, physics cannot work. Examples: length of a table, mass of rice, temperature of water.

💡 Key Idea: Physics is only as good as the measurements behind it.

2.2 Physical Quantities

📌 Physical Quantity: Anything that can be measured and expressed with a number and a unit.

Examples: Length (5m), Mass (10kg), Time (30s), Temperature (25°C).

2.7 Accuracy vs Precision

🎯 Accuracy: Closeness to true value
📊 Precision: Consistency of measurements

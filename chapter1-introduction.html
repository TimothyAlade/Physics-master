Chapter 1: Introduction to Physics

📘 SS1 First Term ⏱️ Reading: 25 mins

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Define physics and explain its meaning
  • Identify and describe the main branches of physics
  • Explain the importance of physics in everyday life
  • Distinguish between fundamental and derived quantities
  • Identify common measuring instruments and their uses

1.1 Meaning of Physics

📌 Definition: Physics is the branch of science that studies matter, energy, and the interactions between them.

The word "Physics" comes from the Greek word "Physikos" meaning "nature". Physics is called the "fundamental science" because it forms the foundation for other sciences.

1.2 Branches of Physics

BranchWhat It StudiesExamples
MechanicsMotion, forces, and energyMoving cars, falling objects
Heat & ThermodynamicsTemperature, heat transferEngines, refrigerators
OpticsLight and visionMirrors, lenses
Electricity & MagnetismCharges, currents, fieldsCircuits, motors
Waves & SoundWave motion, soundMusic, echoes
Atomic & Nuclear PhysicsAtoms, radioactivityX-rays, nuclear power

1.3 Importance of Physics

🏠 At Home

  • Refrigerator (thermodynamics)
  • Microwave (electromagnetic waves)
  • Electric iron (energy conversion)

🚗 Transportation

  • Cars (Newton's laws)
  • Aeroplanes (Bernoulli's principle)
  • Ships (buoyancy)

📱 Technology

  • Mobile phones (EM waves)
  • Computers (semiconductors)
  • GPS (relativity)
💡 Key Point: Physics is everywhere! Understanding physics opens doors to careers in engineering, medicine, and technology.

1.5 Scalars and Vectors

📌 Scalar: Magnitude only (mass 5kg, speed 60km/h)
📌 Vector: Magnitude + direction (velocity 60km/h East, force 10N down)

📝 Chapter 1 Summary

  • Physics studies matter, energy, and their interactions
  • Main branches: Mechanics, Thermodynamics, Optics, Electricity, Waves, Atomic
  • Scalars have magnitude only; vectors have magnitude and direction

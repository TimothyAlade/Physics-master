Chapter 13: Magnetism

📗 SS2 Second Term ⏱️ Reading: 30 mins

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Define magnets and magnetic materials
  • Describe magnetic poles and their properties
  • Explain magnetic fields and field lines
  • Distinguish between magnetic and non-magnetic materials
  • Explain methods of magnetization and demagnetization
  • Describe Earth's magnetism

13.1 What is Magnetism?

📌 Magnetism: A force of attraction or repulsion produced by magnets or moving electric charges.
📌 Magnet: An object that produces a magnetic field and attracts magnetic materials like iron, nickel, and cobalt.

Magnetic Materials

13.2 Magnetic Poles

📌 Magnetic Poles: Regions of a magnet where the magnetic force is strongest. Every magnet has a North (N) pole and a South (S) pole.
🧲 Law of Magnetic Poles:
• Like poles repel (N-N or S-S)
• Unlike poles attract (N-S)
• A single magnetic pole (monopole) does NOT exist

13.3 Magnetic Field

📌 Magnetic Field: The region around a magnet where magnetic forces can be felt or detected. Represented by magnetic field lines.

Properties of Magnetic Field Lines:

  • Go from North to South outside the magnet
  • Go from South to North inside the magnet
  • Form closed loops
  • Never cross each other
  • Closer lines = stronger field

13.4 Types of Magnets

TypeDescriptionExamples
Permanent MagnetsRetain magnetism for long timeBar magnet, horseshoe magnet, compass needle
Temporary MagnetsLose magnetism quicklyElectromagnet, soft iron core
ElectromagnetsMagnetism from electric currentElectric bell, relay, MRI machine

13.5 Magnetization and Demagnetization

Methods of Magnetization

  • Stroking: Rubbing a magnet along a magnetic material in one direction
  • Electrical Method: Placing material in a solenoid with DC current
  • Induction: Bringing a magnet near a magnetic material

Methods of Demagnetization

  • Heating: Heating the magnet strongly
  • Hammering: Hammering the magnet repeatedly
  • AC Current: Passing alternating current through a solenoid around the magnet

💡 Memory Tip: Soft iron = Soft (easy to magnetize AND demagnetize). Steel = Stubborn (hard to magnetize AND demagnetize).

13.7 Earth's Magnetism

📌 Earth's Magnetic Field: The Earth behaves like a giant bar magnet with a magnetic field that protects us from solar winds.
  • Geographic North ≈ Magnetic South (where compass N points)
  • Angle of Declination: Angle between geographic north and magnetic north
  • Angle of Dip: Angle between Earth's magnetic field and the horizontal
🧭 Compass: A small magnet free to rotate. Its North pole points toward Earth's magnetic South (near geographic North).

📝 Chapter 13 Summary

  • Magnets attract iron, nickel, cobalt (ferromagnetic materials)
  • Like poles repel; unlike poles attract
  • Magnetic field lines: N → S outside, S → N inside
  • Electromagnet: Magnetism from electric current
  • Soft iron: Temporary magnets; Steel: Permanent magnets
  • Earth has a magnetic field (protects from solar wind)

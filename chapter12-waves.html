Chapter 12: Waves

📗 SS2 First Term ⏱️ Reading: 35 mins

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Define waves and describe their types
  • Distinguish between transverse and longitudinal waves
  • Explain wave properties (wavelength, frequency, amplitude, speed)
  • Apply the wave equation (v = fλ)
  • Describe sound waves and their properties
  • Explain echo, reverberation, and applications of sound

12.1 What is a Wave?

📌 Wave: A disturbance that transfers energy from one point to another without the permanent transfer of matter.

Waves carry energy, not matter. The particles of the medium vibrate but do not travel with the wave.

12.2 Types of Waves

Transverse Waves

📌 Transverse Wave: Particles vibrate perpendicular (at right angles) to the direction of wave travel.

Examples: Light waves, water ripples, waves on a string, electromagnetic waves.

Longitudinal Waves

📌 Longitudinal Wave: Particles vibrate parallel (in the same direction) to the direction of wave travel.

Examples: Sound waves, ultrasound, seismic P-waves, slinky spring compression.

12.4 The Wave Equation

v = fλ

Wave speed = Frequency × Wavelength

📐 Example 1: A wave has frequency 50 Hz and wavelength 2 m. Find speed.
v = 50 × 2 = 100 m/s
📐 Example 2: Sound travels at 340 m/s with frequency 170 Hz. Find wavelength.
λ = v/f = 340/170 = 2 m
📐 Example 3: Radio waves (speed 3×10⁸ m/s) with wavelength 300 m. Find frequency.
f = v/λ = 3×10⁸/300 = 1,000,000 Hz (1 MHz)

12.5 Sound Waves

📌 Sound: A longitudinal mechanical wave that travels through a medium (solid, liquid, or gas). Cannot travel through vacuum.

Properties of Sound:

  • Speed in air: ~340 m/s (at 20°C)
  • Speed in water: ~1500 m/s
  • Speed in steel: ~5000 m/s
  • Audible range: 20 Hz – 20,000 Hz
PropertyRelated to
PitchFrequency (high f = high pitch)
LoudnessAmplitude (large A = loud)
Quality/TimbreWaveform (harmonics)

12.6 Echo and Reverberation

📌 Echo: Reflection of sound that arrives at the listener after a distinct delay (≥ 0.1 seconds).
📌 Reverberation: Multiple reflections of sound that blend together (persistence of sound).
📐 Echo Example: Sound reflects off a wall 170 m away. How long for echo?
Total distance = 2 × 170 = 340 m
Time = 340/340 = 1 second

12.7 Applications of Sound Waves

  • Sonar: Detecting objects underwater using sound reflection
  • Ultrasound: Medical imaging (pregnancy scans), cleaning
  • Seismology: Studying earthquakes using seismic waves
  • Musical instruments: Producing sound through vibration

📝 Chapter 12 Summary

  • Transverse waves: Particles vibrate perpendicular (light, water)
  • Longitudinal waves: Particles vibrate parallel (sound)
  • Wave equation: v = fλ
  • Frequency & Period: f = 1/T
  • Sound: Longitudinal wave, speed ~340 m/s in air
  • Echo: Reflected sound heard after delay

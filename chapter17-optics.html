Chapter 17: Optics

📙 SS3 First Term ⏱️ Reading: 35 mins

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • State the laws of reflection
  • Describe image formation by plane and curved mirrors
  • State the laws of refraction (Snell's Law)
  • Explain total internal reflection and critical angle
  • Describe lenses and image formation
  • Apply the lens formula (1/f = 1/u + 1/v)

17.1 Reflection of Light

📌 Reflection: The bouncing back of light when it strikes a surface.

Laws of Reflection

  • Angle of incidence (i) = Angle of reflection (r)
  • The incident ray, reflected ray, and normal all lie in the same plane

Types of Reflection

Regular ReflectionDiffuse Reflection
Smooth surface (mirror)Rough surface (wall, paper)
Parallel rays remain parallelRays scatter in different directions
Clear image formedNo clear image

17.2 Mirrors

Plane Mirror

  • Image is virtual (behind mirror)
  • Image is same size as object
  • Image is laterally inverted (left-right reversed)
  • Image distance = Object distance

Curved Mirrors

Concave MirrorConvex Mirror
Curves inwardCurves outward
Can form real or virtual imagesAlways forms virtual images
Magnifying, shaving mirrorsCar side mirrors, security
Converges lightDiverges light

17.3 Refraction of Light

📌 Refraction: The bending of light as it passes from one medium to another due to change in speed.

Snell's Law

n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂

Where: n = refractive index, θ = angle (from the normal)

n = speed in vacuum / speed in medium = c / v
📐 Example: Light enters glass (n=1.5) from air (n=1.0) at 30° to the normal. Find angle of refraction.
sin θ₂ = (1.0 × sin 30°) / 1.5 = 0.5/1.5 = 0.333
θ₂ = 19.5° (light bends toward the normal)

17.4 Total Internal Reflection & Critical Angle

📌 Critical Angle (θc): The angle of incidence above which total internal reflection occurs (light goes from denser to less dense medium).
📌 Total Internal Reflection: 100% of light reflects back into the denser medium when i > θc.
sin θc = n₂ / n₁
📐 Example: Light traveling from glass (n=1.5) to air (n=1.0). Find critical angle.
sin θc = 1.0/1.5 = 0.667
θc = 41.8°

Applications of Total Internal Reflection:

  • Optical fibers: Internet, medical endoscopes
  • Prisms: Periscopes, binoculars (reflect light by 90° or 180°)
  • Diamond sparkle: High refractive index = small critical angle = lots of TIR

17.5 Lenses

Convex (Converging) LensConcave (Diverging) Lens
Thicker in middleThinner in middle
Converges light raysDiverges light rays
Real or virtual imagesAlways virtual images
Magnifying glass, camera, eyeCorrecting short-sightedness

The Lens Formula

1/f = 1/u + 1/v

Where: f = focal length (m), u = object distance (m), v = image distance (m)

Sign Convention (Real-is-Positive):

  • Real object: u is positive
  • Real image: v is positive (opposite side of lens)
  • Virtual image: v is negative (same side as object)
  • Convex lens: f is positive
  • Concave lens: f is negative
Magnification M = v/u = hi/ho
📐 Example: Object 30 cm from convex lens (f=15 cm). Find image position and magnification.
1/v = 1/f - 1/u = 1/15 - 1/30 = 2/30 - 1/30 = 1/30
v = 30 cm (real image, same side as object)
M = 30/30 = 1 (same size)

📝 Chapter 17 Summary

  • Reflection: i = r (plane mirror: virtual, same size, laterally inverted)
  • Refraction: n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂ (Snell's Law)
  • Critical Angle: sin θc = n₂/n₁ (TIR when i > θc)
  • Lens Formula: 1/f = 1/u + 1/v
  • Magnification: M = v/u

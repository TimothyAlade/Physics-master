Chapter 15: Current Electricity

📙 SS3 First Term ⏱️ Reading: 40 mins

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Apply Ohm's Law to advanced circuit problems
  • Analyze series and parallel circuits in detail
  • Calculate electrical power and energy
  • Explain electrical safety measures
  • Solve problems involving internal resistance

15.1 Ohm's Law (Advanced Applications)

📌 Ohm's Law: V = IR. The current through a conductor is directly proportional to the potential difference across it, provided temperature and other physical conditions remain constant.
V = IR  |  I = V/R  |  R = V/I
📐 Example: A 20 Ω resistor has 5 V across it. Find the current.
I = V/R = 5/20 = 0.25 A

15.2 Series Circuits

Components connected end-to-end. Same current flows through all components.
  • Current: I = I₁ = I₂ = I₃ (same everywhere)
  • Voltage: V = V₁ + V₂ + V₃ (divides)
  • Resistance: R = R₁ + R₂ + R₃
📐 Example: 4 Ω, 6 Ω, and 10 Ω in series with 40 V supply. Find total R, current, and voltage across each.
R = 4 + 6 + 10 = 20 Ω
I = V/R = 40/20 = 2 A
V₁ = 2×4=8V, V₂ = 2×6=12V, V₃ = 2×10=20V

15.3 Parallel Circuits

Components connected across same two points. Same voltage across all branches.
  • Voltage: V = V₁ = V₂ = V₃ (same across all)
  • Current: I = I₁ + I₂ + I₃ (divides)
  • Resistance: 1/R = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + 1/R₃
📐 Example: 3 Ω and 6 Ω in parallel with 12 V supply. Find total R, total I, and current in each branch.
1/R = 1/3 + 1/6 = 3/6 → R = 2 Ω
I = 12/2 = 6 A
I₁ = 12/3 = 4A, I₂ = 12/6 = 2A

15.4 Electrical Power

📌 Electrical Power (P): The rate at which electrical energy is converted to other forms. SI Unit: Watt (W).
P = VI = I²R = V²/R
📐 Example: A 60 W bulb operates at 240 V. Find the current and resistance.
I = P/V = 60/240 = 0.25 A
R = V/I = 240/0.25 = 960 Ω

15.5 Electrical Energy

📌 Electrical Energy (E): The work done by an electric current. SI Unit: Joule (J).
Commercial unit: Kilowatt-hour (kWh)
E = Pt = VIt = I²Rt = (V²/R)t
📐 Example: A 1000 W heater runs for 2 hours. Find energy consumed in kWh.
E = 1000 × 2 = 2000 Wh = 2 kWh

15.6 Internal Resistance

📌 Internal Resistance (r): The resistance within a cell or battery that opposes current flow. It causes the terminal voltage to be less than the EMF when current flows.
E = I(R + r)  or  E = V + Ir

Where: E = EMF (V), V = terminal voltage, I = current, R = external resistance, r = internal resistance

📐 Example: A battery of EMF 12 V and internal resistance 0.5 Ω is connected to a 5.5 Ω resistor. Find current.
I = E/(R+r) = 12/(5.5+0.5) = 12/6 = 2 A

15.7 Electrical Safety

Safety DeviceFunction
FuseMelts when current exceeds rated value, breaking circuit
Circuit BreakerAutomatically switches off when overload detected
Earth WireProvides low-resistance path to ground, preventing shock
Double InsulationTwo layers of insulation protecting user from live parts

📝 Chapter 15 Summary

  • Ohm's Law: V = IR
  • Series: Same I, V divides, R = R₁+R₂
  • Parallel: Same V, I divides, 1/R = 1/R₁+1/R₂
  • Power: P = VI = I²R = V²/R
  • Energy: E = Pt = VIt (Joules or kWh)
  • Internal Resistance: E = I(R+r), V = E - Ir
